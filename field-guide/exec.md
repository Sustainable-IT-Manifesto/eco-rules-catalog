## Page 1 — The Core Idea

Waste hides in systems that are “working.”

If:

* CPU is idle
* Latency is creeping
* Storage grows forever
* Costs rise quietly
* Retries spike during incidents

You have structural inefficiency.

---

## Page 2 — The Four Tiers

**Tier 1 — Local waste**
Developer-level inefficiency.

**Tier 2 — Service amplification**
Network, retries, timeouts.

**Tier 3 — Structural waste**
Architecture, scaling, storage.

**Tier 4 — Cultural waste**
No budgets, no baselines, no ownership.

---

## Page 3 — The Three Questions

1. What are we paying for that we’re not using?
2. Where does failure amplify?
3. What are we storing forever?

---

## Page 4 — Fast Wins (30 Days)

* Add timeouts
* Add retry backoff
* Add retention policy
* Right-size one service
* Add performance baseline

---

## Page 5 — Architecture Moves (Quarter)

* Add autoscaling guardrails
* Reduce sync dependency chains
* Introduce lifecycle governance
* Consolidate duplicate services

---

## Page 6 — AI Efficiency Moves

* Route models by need
* Cap context
* Batch inference
* Monitor GPU utilization

---

## Page 7 — Governance Moves

* Define performance budget
* Track cost by service
* Assign sustainability owner

---

## Page 8 — The Measurement Rule

Before → Change → After

No baseline = no improvement.

## Page 9 — What This Isn’t

Not austerity.

Better code.
